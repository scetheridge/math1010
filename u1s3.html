1.3 Budgeting

  1. The table below represents Jack’s average income and expenses for one month. Answer the following questions related to the table.
    Income Expenses
    Salary: $2500 Groceries: $420
    Utilities: $150
    Gasoline: $100
    Phone and Internet: $160
    Insurance: $115
    Rent: $650
    Entertainment: $400
    1. What percentage of his salary is Jack spending on rent?

      \( \frac{650}{2500}=.26=26\%\)

    2. What percentage of his salary is Jack spending on entertainment?

      \( \frac{400}{2500}=.16=16\%\)

    3. What is the ratio of food costs to the cost of utilities in simplest form?

      \( \frac{420}{150}=\frac{14}{5}\)

    4. What is Jack’s monthly cash flow?

      Income-Expenses= $2500-$1995=$505

    5. If Jack’s landlord raises his rent to $700 per month, what percent increase does this represent?

      \( \frac{700-650}{650}=\frac{50}{650}=.0769=7.69\% \)
