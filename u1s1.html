  5. The Trail Mix Calorie Counter gives the following information on its website. Use the pattern in the table to answer the questions.
    Grams of trail mix Calories
    50 220
    150 660
    300 1320
    500 2200
    1. Eddie eats 75 grams of trail mix. How many calories does he eat?

      2. \(\frac{50\;grams}{220\;calories}=\frac{75\;grams}{x\;calories}\)

      \(50x=16,500\)

      \(\frac{\displaystyle50x}{50}=\frac{\displaystyle16,500}{50}\)

      \(x=330\)

      Eddies eats 330 calories

    2. Claire eats trail mix containing 1000 calories. How many grams of trail mix does she eat?

      3. \(\frac{50\;grams}{220\;calories}=\frac{x\;grams}{1000\;calories}\)

      \(50,000=220x\)

      \(\frac{\displaystyle50,000}{220}=\frac{\displaystyle220x}{220}\)

      \(x=227\)

      Claire eats 227 grams

  6. When she drives to work, Yvonne travels 10 miles in about 15 minutes. Rico travels 23 miles in about 30 minutes. Who has the faster average speed?

    Yvonne: \(10/15= 0.67\) miles/minute

    Rico: \(23/30= 0.77\) miles/minute

    Rico is driving faster.

  7. On the winding mountain road to Elkmont campground, visitors travel only 6 miles in 10 minutes.
    1. At this speed, how long does it take a visitor to travel 18 miles?

      \(\frac{6\;miles}{10\;minutes}=\frac{18\;miles}{x\;minutes}\)

      \(6x=180\)

      \(x=30\)

      It takes 30 minutes to travel 18 miles.

    2. At this speed, how far do visitors go in 15 minutes?

      \(\frac{6\;miles}{10\;minutes}=\frac{x\;miles}{15\;minutes}\)

      \(90=10x\)

      \(x=9\)

      The visitor can go 9 miles in 15 minutes.

  9. The Transitional Studies Department can purchase calculators in bulk at a price of $1000 for 20. At this rate, how many calculators can they purchase for $1250?

    \(\frac{$1000}{20\;calculators}=\frac{$1250}{x\;calculators}\)

    \(1000x=25,000\)

    \(x=25\)

    They can purchase 25 calculators.

  10. Sasha gives vitamins to her dogs. The recommended dosage is 2 teaspoons per day for adult dogs weighing 20 pounds. She needs to give vitamins to Campbell who weighs 75 pounds and to Rosie who weighs 7 pounds. What is the correct dosage for each dog?

    Campbell: \(\frac{2\;teaspoons}{20\;pounds}=\frac{x\;teaspoons}{75\;pounds}\)

    \(20x=150\)

    \(x=7.5\)

    Campbell's dosage is 7.5 teaspoons.

    Rosie: \(\frac{2\;teaspoons}{20\;pounds}=\frac{x\;teaspoons}{7\;pounds}\)

    \(20x=14\)

    \(x=.7\)

    Rosie's dosage is 0.7 teaspoons.

  11. Suzanne and Nancy walk 1 mile in 15 minutes. They can keep up this pace for several hours.

      \(x=6\)

      They walk 6 miles in an hour and a half.

    2. About how far do they walk in 65 minutes?

      \(\frac{1\;mile}{15\;minutes}=\frac{x\;miles}{65\;minutes}\)

      \(15x=65\)

      \(x=4.3\)

      They walk 4.3 miles in 65 minutes.
